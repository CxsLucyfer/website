Today marks the 7th birthday of Helperbird, and we are excited to celebrate with all of our users!

Over the past year, we've been hard at work adding tons of new features and making improvements to the extension, and we think you're going to love what we've come up with.

From our redesigned Reader feature to our new auto scrolling and voice typing or dictation capabilities, we've got something for everyone. Word prediction is revamped and with Google Docs support

With Helperbird's word prediction, users can type faster and more accurately in Google Docs, saving time and boosting their productivity. The feature uses advanced machine learning algorithms to predict the next word the user is likely to type, based on the context of the document and the user's writing style. We also added a speaker button next to each word.

"We are always looking for ways to help our users work more efficiently and effectively," said Robert James,Founder of Helperbird. "With the addition of word prediction, we think Helperbird just got a whole lot better."

To use the new word prediction feature, users simply need to install Helperbird and enable the feature in their settings. It works seamlessly in the background, providing suggestions as users type in Google Docs.

Helperbird's word prediction is available now, and can be found in the latest version of the extension, available for free from the Chrome Web Store, Edge Web Store or iOs Store.

Exciting news! Our team at Helperbird has just released a new feature: auto scrolling.

No more tedious mouse clicking to get to the bottom of a long document - just sit back and let Helperbird do the work for you.

Enable the feature in your settings and let the smooth, seamless scrolling take you to new heights of productivity. Try it out now and let us know what you think!

Introducing the all-new Reader Mode feature in Helperbird, now with customizable themes!

Attention all hands-free enthusiasts: the Voice typing/dictation feature in Helperbird has been rebuilt and is better than ever!
